Playtest Day — swap games with classmates

Learning Intentions

  • I can play another student's game and give useful feedback
  • I can receive feedback and act on it
  • I can fix 2 bugs in my own game based on peer feedback

Success Criteria

  • I've played at least 2 classmates' games
  • I've given written feedback on the feedback sheet
  • I've fixed 2+ bugs in my own game
5 min

Do Now — Error of the Day

Set up your game for playtest: make sure it runs, copy your folder to a USB or share folder.

10 min

I Do — Live-coded demo

Teacher demos the playtest sheet. 3 questions only: (1) What did you enjoy? (specific feature) (2) What was confusing? (specific moment) (3) What's one thing you'd change? (concrete suggestion)

15 min

We Do — Guided build

Rotate stations every 10 min. Play classmate's game. Fill in their feedback sheet. Next station. Three rotations = 30 minutes of play-and-feedback.

35 min

You Do — Your turn

Return to your own laptop. Read the 2-3 feedback sheets you received. Pick the 2 most common or most useful criticisms. Fix them in your code in the remaining 30 minutes.

Support

You play 1 game (not 2); you can focus on giving feedback to a buddy's game.

Core

Full rotation — 2-3 games played + 2 bugs fixed.

Extension

Offer to help another student debug their game; your role is 'code reviewer'.

5 min

Exit Ticket — Coding Journal

Journal: what did a classmate tell you about your game that surprised you?

📝 Journal submission

Submit your 2-3 sentence journal entry in Google Classroom before you leave. Every entry contributes to your Coding Journal (/5) in the final assessment.

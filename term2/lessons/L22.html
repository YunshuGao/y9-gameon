L22 · Week 11 ⏱ 75 minutes 🎯 IND5-5 · IND5-7 · IND5-8 📂 Phase 4 — Polish, Twist & Showcase

Showcase + written evaluation

Learning Intentions

  • I can write a clear 500-word evaluation of my game
  • I can reflect on what I learned about programming
  • I can submit a complete assessment package

Success Criteria

  • My evaluation addresses all 5 evaluation questions
  • My final game zip is submitted to Google Classroom
  • My journal has 10+ entries
5 min

Do Now — Error of the Day

Open your journal. Read your Week 1 entry. What's different about what you can do now vs Week 1?

10 min

I Do — Live-coded demo

Teacher models the evaluation writing: opens the 5 questions, writes a sample answer to #1 live. Emphasises: be specific. Use line numbers from your code. Screenshot key moments. Show the video option: a 2-minute phone-recorded walkthrough.

15 min

We Do — Guided build

Together: write the opening paragraph (~100 words) of the evaluation. What does your game do? What is your twist? Why did you choose it?

35 min

You Do — Your turn

Write the remaining 400 words (OR record the 2-min video). Then ZIP your game folder. Submit to Google Classroom: (1) game.zip, (2) journal.docx, (3) evaluation.docx or video link.

Support

Use the evaluation_template.docx — fill in the 5 sections. 300 words minimum.

Core

500 words OR 2-min video. Full 5 questions.

Extension

Write a 700-word evaluation AND a 60-sec hype video. Link both.

5 min

Exit Ticket — Coding Journal

Final journal entry: in 3 sentences, what will you remember about this term?

📝 Journal submission

Submit your 2-3 sentence journal entry in Google Classroom before you leave. Every entry contributes to your Coding Journal (/5) in the final assessment.

YOUR TWIST — one original feature

Learning Intentions

  • I can plan and implement one original feature in my game
  • I can document my feature with a code comment
  • I can use pygame docs to find functions I don't know

Success Criteria

  • My game has something no other student's game has
  • I've documented my twist at the top of my main .py file
  • My twist WORKS — no crashes
5 min

Do Now — Error of the Day

Open the Your Twist ideas page. Pick ONE idea (or propose your own to teacher). Write it in your journal.

10 min

I Do — Live-coded demo

Teacher picks ONE twist idea from the list (e.g. Power-Up) and live-codes a skeleton. Shows how to: search pygame docs, try a hypothesis, fail, adjust. Emphasise: your code will break. That is NORMAL. Debug it.

15 min

We Do — Guided build

In pairs — buddy check: explain your twist idea in 60 seconds. Your buddy asks 2 questions. Swap. Refine your plan based on the questions.

35 min

You Do — Your turn

Implement your twist. 35 minutes. Teacher circulates to help with debugging. If you finish early: add a second twist, or polish your visuals, or add sound.

Support

Pick from the pre-designed twist list (sound pack, boss, or power-up). Use starter code provided.

Core

Pick or propose. Implement from scratch with teacher guidance.

Extension

Propose your own novel twist. Research solution via pygame docs or similar open-source game code.

5 min

Exit Ticket — Coding Journal

Journal: describe your twist in 3 sentences. What does it add to the game?

📝 Journal submission

Submit your 2-3 sentence journal entry in Google Classroom before you leave. Every entry contributes to your Coding Journal (/5) in the final assessment.
